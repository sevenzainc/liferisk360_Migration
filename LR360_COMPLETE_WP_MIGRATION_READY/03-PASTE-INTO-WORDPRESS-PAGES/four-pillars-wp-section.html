Four Pillars

The four connected pillars that structure the LifeRisk360 model.

Youth-ready design

Four pillars that help youth move forward

Each pillar helps youth understand risk, connect it to purpose, build readiness, and receive the right support.

Pillar

Life-Risk Awareness

Youth identify risks in personal safety, digital life, relationships, substances, gangs, gambling, AI, cybersecurity, emotional pressure, and poor planning.

Desired outcome: Youth become aware of risks and how these risks affect goals.

Pillar

Life Direction and Goal Setting

Youth define goals across education, career, finances, health, relationships, values, and community contribution.

Desired outcome: Youth connect daily choices to long-term direction.

Pillar

Economic Readiness

Youth develop money awareness, saving habits, work readiness, entrepreneurship, digital skills, and scam resistance.

Desired outcome: Youth become better prepared to participate in the economy.

Pillar

Support, Mitigation, and Accountability

Trusted stakeholders help youth reduce exposure, track actions, and improve readiness through role-based support.

Desired outcome: Youth receive structured support without shame or unnecessary exposure.

Turn the model into action

Use the four pillars to guide youth awareness, goal setting, economic readiness, and stakeholder-supported mitigation.
