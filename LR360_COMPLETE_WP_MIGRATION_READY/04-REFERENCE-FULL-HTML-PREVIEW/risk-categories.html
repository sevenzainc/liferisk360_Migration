Expanded Risk Groups

Political

Political and Civic Influence Risks

Helps youth evaluate misinformation, polarization, civic pressure, unsafe participation, and harmful recruitment content while keeping the platform politically neutral.

Data Privacy

Data Privacy Risks

Helps youth protect personal information, location, app permissions, identity, and data shared with AI tools or online platforms.

Gender

Gender and Identity Risks

Supports respect, dignity, boundaries, privacy, and safe conversation around identity pressure, bullying, and disclosure.

Health

Health and Wellness Risks

Focuses on sleep, energy, movement, reliable health information, and knowing when to ask for appropriate support.

Social Media

Social Media Risks

Addresses comparison pressure, viral challenges, oversharing, influencer pressure, and online conflict escalation.

Risk Rating Approach

Low Awareness

The youth may not fully understand the risk.

Developing Awareness

The youth is beginning to understand the risk but may need support.

Moderate Awareness

The youth understands the risk but may still need guidance.

Strong Awareness

The youth can identify the risk and explain safer choices.

Action Ready

The youth understands the risk and has practical steps to reduce exposure.
